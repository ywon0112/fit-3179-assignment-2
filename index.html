The Lifeblood of a Nation

Water and Wealth in the Murray-Darling Basin (2020)

By Wong Yie Meng

Published: October 21, 2025

The Murray-Darling Basin (MDB) is the agricultural heartland of Australia, producing a significant portion of the nation's food and fibre. However, its water resources are limited and highly variable. This visualization explores the crucial relationship between where water naturally falls (Rainfall - the "Cause"), how this water is utilized by different farming industries (Water Use - the "Action"), and the resulting economic output (Agricultural Value - the "Effect"). Discover how different regions within the basin adapted to the conditions of 2020.

Explore the Data

The map above shows Australia's Murray-Darling Basin agricultural regions. White areas represent the MDB states where our data is focused, gray areas show other Australian states for geographic context, and the blue background represents the ocean. Each colored bubble on the map represents a catchment region. The colour of each bubble indicates the average annual rainfall received in 2020 (lighter blue = less rain, darker blue = more), representing the natural water 'income'. The size of each bubble represents the total economic value ($AUD million) generated from irrigated agriculture in that year, showing the economic 'output'. Notice regions like VIC Murray Below ($1544M) and SA Murray ($879M) generated immense value despite being among the driest areas, receiving only ~200mm rainfall. Click a bubble to select it. This will highlight its position in the overview scatter plot below and filter the two bar charts to show only that region's data.

This scatter plot provides a basin-wide overview for 2020. Each point represents one MDB region. Its position shows the relationship between Rainfall (X-axis) and the Total Economic Value generated (Y-axis). The size of the point indicates the Total Water Use (ML) for that region. Observe the outliers demonstrating the core story: regions like VIC Murray Below (202mm rain, $1544M value, 669k ML used), SA Murray (204mm rain, $879M value, 340k ML used), and NSW Murrumbidgee (304mm rain, $711M value, 546k ML used) achieve high value despite low-to-moderate rainfall, often correlating with significant water use (large point size). Click a bubble on the map above to see detailed breakdowns.

Key Insights

Across the Murray-Darling Basin, agricultural success in 2020 wasn't simply dictated by rainfall. The data clearly shows how strategic water management allows regions to overcome natural limitations. VIC Murray Below stands out, generating the highest value ($1544M) with the highest water use (669,124 ML) despite receiving only 202mm of rain. Similarly, VIC Goulburn-Broken ($1042M value from 399mm rain and 364,252 ML water use) and NSW Murrumbidgee ($712M value from 304mm rain and 546,256 ML water use) highlight how irrigation supports high-value industries, significantly boosting output beyond what rainfall alone would permit. This delicate balance is central to the MDB's agricultural economy.

Data Source

The data for this project was sourced from the Australian Bureau of Agricultural and Resource Economics and Sciences (ABARES). The original dataset, "Murray-Darling Basin water market dataset", is publicly available from:

https://www.agriculture.gov.au/abares/research-topics/water/mdb-water-market-dataset

Data Processing

The following original files were cleaned, processed, and combined into a single dataset (`mdb_2020_data_with_coords.csv`) for use in this visualisation:

  • MDBWaterMarketCatchmentDataset_Demand_v1.0.0.xlsx - Demand - Area and Water use.csv
  • MDBWaterMarketCatchmentDataset_Demand_v1.0.0.xlsx - Demand - Production and Value.csv
  • MDBWaterMarketCatchmentDataset_Supply_v1.0.0.xlsx - Supply.csv
